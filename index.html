LIMO-Velo: LiDAR-Inertial SLAM for fast and aggressive movement

September 2021 - March 2022 @ HKUST as a Visiting Intern Researcher


Based on Fast-LIO's approach, this framework corrects the localization with LiDAR observations at an extremely high-frequency (up to 200Hz) and maps directly to an Incremental KD-Tree at low-frequency (10Hz). Allowing us to post-process the pointcloud accordingly (remove dynamic objects or apply segmentation) while not losing any quality w.r.t. to mapping at 200Hz.

We call it “Localize Intensively and Map Offline” (L.I.M.O.) and instead of waiting for the full pointcloud, it uses the points as a continuous stream. This allows us to go at high velocities (20m/s) and aggressive turns (120deg/s) with a centimeter-level accurate dense map.

I was advised by the most talented research mentor Qifeng Chen throughout the whole process and I published the code online for anyone to use and improve on: https://github.com/Huguet57/LIMO-Velo/.

Main contributions

  • + First algorithm using the fact that spinning LiDARs can localize at higher frequency than mapping without loss in quality.
  • + With higher localization frequency, the steps are smaller, less complex and better approximated by EKF (since it assumes quasi-linearity).
  • + More accurate motion compensation that can be smoothed out and processed when mapping.
  • + Ability to post-process what it maps while being real-time.


XALOC: Spain’s first ever driverless vehicle

September 2020 - September 2021 @ UPC as a Student


XALOC’s journey began in 2018 when within a full year, extremely talented engineers built the first DV vehicle ever seen in Spain and finished 10 laps in an official Formula Student Driverless competition (in 20 minutes).

After the pandemy hit Spain the hardest, the project had to be stopped in March 2020 and we resumed it in September 2020 with a clear goal in mind: reach 90km/h with a reliable autonomous solution.

In May 2021, the car reached its highest velocities of 60km/h before disaster strucked: the brake microcontroller collapsed due to high fatigue. With the competitions in less than two months, the electronics issues of the car made it unable to complete any official competition lap. I know what it feels like to fail big time, that's innovation!


My roles

  • Localization and Mapping Lead

    • Developed a FastSLAM algorithm to keep track of the cones as landmarks. Additionaly, modified the A-LOAM (Dense SLAM) source code to remove the dependence on GPS tracking as first solution of the FastSLAM algorithm.

  • LiDAR filtering

    • Using a clever combination of grids and filtering techniques, the car was able to see cones at 30 meters distance with a single Velodyne 32C scan.

  • Self-calibration algorithm

    • Using a YOLO network to detect cones with our two cameras, we could estimate the extrinsic parameters of the LiDAR and two cameras via gradient descent.

  • AI-Enabled backup pipeline

    • If the cameras should fail (or a beam of light blinded them), an camera-independent pipeline was developed to identify the color of cones. A 3d-CNN with >90% accuracy for cones in a 12-meter radius.


This was my first time working with SLAM and it inspired me to develop my own SLAM algorithm filling all the blanks the current open-source State-Of-The-Art offered.
